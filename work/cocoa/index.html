Cocoa

Cocoa was an idea incubation app based on my experience designing insurance software. I wanted to test out some ideas regarding where commercial insurance software needed to go to appeal to the next generation of insurance agents: Gen Z and beyond.

About

Cocoa was a personal project that I worked on outside of EZLynx. I was frustrated with the lack of progress in commercial insurance software, particularly it's focus on desktop only users. I had my design team do some research into what the next generation of insurance agents would look like. I had also done some competitive research into commercial insurance software.

EZLynx' software at the time was complex and desktop based, which was fine for an older audience (millenial, Gen X, etc.) but would lose market share over time to newer competitors who were appealing to a younger audience (e.g. Gen Z).

The EZLynx 5 redesign that I had initiated was designed to solve that problem for the personal insurance products on the EZLynx platform: the design was modern, responsive, and far more appealing to a younger audience. The commercial insurance products were still desktop only and were not initially part of the EZLynx 5 redesign.

There was no official roadmap to move the commercial products to EZLynx 5 or to mobile. I felt that this was too important of a problem to ignore, so I decided to explore it on my own time, with the hope that I could incorporate my learnings into future EZLynx commercial insurance projects.

I leveraged a lot of the research I had already done up to this point for the various other projects I worked on at EZLynx. To ensure this completed this project, I gave myself a time limit of about 1 month.

I used the following process.

Meet Maria

Maria is a young insurance agent. She’s completed her training and is looking for a way to earn a living as an agent. Maria didn’t choose to become an agent. She did so because there wasn’t enough money coming from freelancing as an Uber driver, or running tasks using Task Rabbit. She wants a more steady source of income. Her mother was an insurance agent and she recommended Maria at least try being an agent. After going through the training she’s ready to at least try being an agent.

Maria wants minimum on-boarding, and maximum productivity from the get-go. She wants a delightful, intuitive, and engaging experience even in a commercial, enterprise app. Maria will not accept sub-par experiences. She demands a higher quality experience.

User Flows

Threads

A typical insurance scenario starts with a customer contacting Maria to request help with insuring their business. I wanted to follow the convention of most messaging apps (Facebook Messenger, WhatsApp, Teams, Slack), where communication from customers are grouped into separate threads.

This allows Maria to see the history of each customer's conversation and offer them better service.

 

Notifications

Since Cocoa is a mobile app, it's tied into the mobile phone's notification system. That means, Cocoa can be notified of any customer request via phone call, text message, or any social media app that can be integrated with Cocoa.

 

New Customer

Duplicate customer data is a major problem in insurance software. That makes it harder to serve a customer because their data gets scattered across multiple customer records if they leave and return to an agent. Cocoa gets around that by letting Maria search for the customer first, so that if they already exist, they can add the quote to the existing customer.

Screens

I went through several iterations before I arrived at the final designs below. The layout is more spacious open than the typical commercial insurance app. Because I was working on a deadline, I used the Roboto rounded icons.

I went through several names trying to find one that would express the intent that I had for this product. I finally settled on Cocoa, because like the food, this app was designed to give users a shot of energy and excitement. I also wanted a name that was organic vs the cold, techy names usually chosen by insurance software companies.

Homepage

The homepage offers Maria an at-a-glance view of how her business is doing. Insurance agents get paid with commissions and agency fees on premiums from customers. Keeping track of that information vs. the number of claims that are eating into their revenue and profits is an important indicator on how Maria’s agency is doing.

Customer Details

Maria can quickly search for any customer and find out about their policy details, claims history, premiums and other information. The app is built around search. Speed is the key.

Chat With Customers

For an insurance agent like Maria with hundreds of customers, it’s very difficult to remember the last thing a customer might have said weeks or months ago. For Maria’s customer though, that is the thing they remember. And they expect their agent to be able to quick respond based on their last converstaion. The Chat view is a Slack-style interface that lets Maria talk to one customer or dozens, and quickly switch context when a customer asks a question.

Maria’s Todos.

Maria has a lot on her mind. Rather than try to juggle hundreds of tasks on pieces of paper or across several apps, Cocoa has a built-in todo manager that helps Maria keep track of her tasks and relate them to the specific customers that they are related to.

Conclusion

Throughout my career, I've worked on personal projects to explore problems that my colleagues or my company are experiencing, but for which there isn't any official budget or roadmap. I then apply the lessons from those projects back at work to move the company forward. Cocoa was my attempt to solve the problem of commercial insurance software for the next generation of users. I was able to apply some of these lessons into migrating some of the commercial products to the modern, responsive EZLynx 5 design that I built.
