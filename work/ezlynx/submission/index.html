Submission Center

There are two types of insurance, personal and commercial insurance.

Personal Insurance Commercial Insurance
Insurance for individuals to cover their homes, cars, motorcycles, boats, etc. Insurance for businesses to insure their places of work, their vehicles, the equipment in their offices, and more.
Insurance agents work on commissions. The commissions on personal insurance policies are very low. Commercial insurance offers much higher commissions.
Agent has to spend every day making dozens of calls so they can earn enough to make a living. Agent only needs to submit a handful of commercial insurance policies a month to make a profit.
Forms for a personal insurance form can usually be submitted in a couple of minutes. Agent has to fill in many complicated forms, contact multiple insurance companies to see which aspect of the business the company will insure, submit the required forms, and then wait weeks or months to find out if each insurance company accepted or rejected their submission.

For commercial insurance agents, keeping track of all these submissions is demanding and time consuming, but also presented a lucrative market opportunity for EZLynx. This was the impetus for Submission Center.

First Pass

Monday: I get blindsided

EZLynx hired a new Product Manager Maverick* to lead the Commercial Submission project. The first problem: Maverick had never worked with a product designer, and didn't feel that one was needed to launch a software product. My manager, Dean*, the VP of Product had seen the positive impact I had had on the product team since joining EZLynx. He insisted that Maverick work with me on this project. Maverick was not happy about this.

Problem #2: Maverick blindsided me with an unexpected announcement — he had scheduled the project to go into development the following week! And he was telling me this on a Monday! I asked Maverick if he could move the development date so I could have more time to work on the designs, but he insisted that he couldn't (or wouldn't). I had less than five days to research, analyze, ideate, and turn around some mockups or wireframes for the development team!

Given the incredibly short time span, I had to plan ahead what I was going to deliver each day (see below). I wouldn't have time to create mockups, so my final deliverable would be a set of wireframes.

Tuesday

Personas

Long before this project started, there were plans in the the EZLynx roadmap to work on more products for commercial insurance agents. I had requested and gotten permission to interview some of those commercial agents. Based on those interviews, I had built a couple of commercial insurance personas.

I was able to use those personas for this project.

Research

I interviewed Maverick and dug deeper into the commercial insurance submission process.

I then met with Maverick to review and iterate over the user flows. I then documented them as shown below.

Thursday

Wireframes

I didn't have time to design any high fidelity mockups, so I put together some wireframes for the development team. I had spent the previous year building a design system using Material Design as a foundation and Angular Material as the component library in collaboration with the front end engineers in the Dallas office where I worked. I knew that the team would be able to use my wireframes to figure out what components to use and where.

Friday

Review

I met with Maverick on Friday to go over the wireframes for Submission Center. I was proud of the work that DJ, Reese, and I had done in such a short time. After showing the wireframes to Michael, he had one last surprise for me: he wasn't going to use my wireframes. He was going to work with the development team on whatever ideas he had already decided on before I started.

I was disappointed, but since Maverick was the product manager for Submission Center, there was nothing I could do. I informed my manager, Dean. He was not happy either, but decided not to pursue it further.

I moved on with my other projects.

Second Pass

A couple of months later, Dean called me into his office. Customers were very upset with the Submission Center product that Maverick had launched. It turns out, the user flow and the interface that Maverick's team designed were terrible. Dean was upset too. Maverick was finally ready to work with me, and without the resistance he had shown the first time. And, I would have a lot more time to work on the designs this time 😁. Dean wanted the design to be right.

Re-examining the user flows

Screens

I took advantage of the additional time to review my mockups with Maverick, my other colleagues in product — including DJ and Reese — and the front end developers in Dallas to make sure the designs I was proposing were feasible and usable. We iterated over the designs over a couple of weeks, and I did some in-house usability testing to validate the flow. Finally, I reviewed the screens with Dean to make sure he was happy with the new designs.

Note that Submission Center product itself is based on the EZLynx 5 design system which I had designed. EZLynx 5 was based on Material Design. The core of the app (top navigation, left navigation, and the left sidebar) were still based on the old EZLynx 4 design.

Conclusion

This project was one of many at EZLynx that demonstrated the value of user experience design and product design as a valuable part of the software development lifecycle. We reviewed the updated designs with our preferred customers and they were enthusiastic about the new design. The new design gives users a birds eye view of all their submissions in one place, and helps the user manage the tangled mess of lines of businesses, customer policies, and insurance providers.

Submission Center went into production and was one of the first real products in EZLynx commercial roadmap.
