Asset Management

Assets are the things that an insurance company can insure. They can be as big as a building, or as small as an earring. When a customer comes to an insurance agent they're typically asking for an auto or home policy. The customer may not be aware that there are additional things in their home or commercial property that can be insured, or perhaps should be insured in case they get lost, stolen, or are destroyed by a fire, flood, or other natural causes.

On the one hand, agents would love to be able to ask customers if they have other things they want to insure. But the process could become lengthy and tedious. And it's possible that the customer may not remember everything that they want to insure.

What if EZLynx could analyze the millions of policies in our database using data science or machine learning and try to find patterns between the kinds of assets a property in a certain area, or of a certain type could have? Could we then prompt the agent to ask the right questions and guide the customer through insuring their assets?

This would give insurance agents an opportunity to increase their revenue from places they traditionally would not even have considered, and it would give EZLynx a new product we could sell.

This was the thought process that led to the Asset Management idea. One of the product managers at EZLynx floated the idea to me, and asked me to explore it further, because they didn't know how to proceed.

I didn't develop any mockups for this project. The goal here was to see if it this was a viable project for EZLynx to pursue.

Lean Business Model Canvas

During this time, I had been researching Lean Startup and collaborative documents that can help teams see if an ideas can be turned into businesses. One such document that I wanted to try out was the Lean Business Model Canvas.

I setup a whiteboarding session with a couple of business analysts I had worked closely with, and together we went through the Lean Business Model Canvas exercise.

Analysis

For the next step, I asked my colleagues who I had worked with on the Lean Business Canvas, and a subject matter expert who had previously worked in an insurance agency, and who I frequently turned to when I needed to deep dive into a complex or obscure aspect of insurance that I couldn't easily obtain any other way.

Our primary persona was going to be a personal insurance agent, with an optional secondary persona being the commercial insurance agent (optional because for commercial insurance, there are local and state laws that specify the kind of insurance a commercial entity must have, so asset management is not usually a problem).

The problem we wanted to solve for was the What, Why, and How:

  • What is the goal of this project. (We had also answered this question during the Lean Business Canvas exercise, so this was mostly to confirm that we were on the right track.)
  • Why do agents need this tool.
  • How could we potentially build this tool in the EZLynx platform.

The most important part of this exercise was the "What Constitutes an Asset" section. This was the hardest problem to solve without our subject matter expert in the room, as we did ot have the decades of experience necessary to build a mental model of the variety of assets that can be insured.

High Level System Flow

The final deliverable for this project was a user flow to show where EZLynx could integrate Asset Management and how agents could earn commissions, which is their primary revenue source.

Once I had all of these artefacts assembled, I put together a presentation for the product manager that initially proposed the idea, to show him how EZLynx could turn the idea into a product, and how agents could earn revenue from it.

Conclusion

With Asset Management, I was able to expand my interest in product beyond user experience and design leadership into the business side of the product lifecycle. Many of the hypotheses we suggested during the lean business model canvas exercise were validated in other projects that implemented aspects of the Asset Management project. And I was able to use my experience on this project to further expand my knowledge of and collaboration with product management.
