Blue Yonder

Blue Yonder delivers a machine learning-driven digital fulfillment platform to enable clients to deliver to their customers when, how and where they want it..

Data Platform

These two apps are part of the Data Platform, which itself is part of the Blue Yonder SaaS platform. To explain all that it’s necessary to take a step back and explain how Blue Yonder got here.

Background

When Blue Yonder first launched its SaaS platform, there were no customer facing interfaces for any of the products on the platform. Any configuration or customization required a highly trained Blue Yonder engineer to do the work. Everything had to be done manually. There was no automation, which meant even the simplest of tasks would take days or weeks.

One of the most egregious examples of this, the one that resulted in Data Platform becoming the first customer facing app in the Blue Yonder cloud, was the problem faced by the Sales people. If a sales person wanted to demo to a customer a simple change to our data model — to show how adaptable the Blue Yonder data platform was — It would take a week for a Blue Yonder engineer to do. Our competitor could do it in hours. This was killing our sales.

A team had been formed to build an app called Data Modeler, which would allow our sales people (and eventually our customers) to make changes to the data model themselves quickly and easily.

Meet the team

This was the team that was originally working on the Data Platform. The names have been changed to protect the innocent.

Raghu was the Product Manager and Product Owner, and Swetlina acted as the Dev Lead and Scrum Master.

The Unthinkable Happens

A few months later, Raghu left the company. For the next six months, there was no product manager, product owner, or project manager for Data Platform. I was the only product person on the team. I was only one who had seen the roadmap, however briefly. The development team was looking to me for guidance.

So, I took the lead.

Taking Charge

I decide to take over as the interim product manager/product owner/product designer until leadership could assign us a new product manager. I helped the development team coordinate with our resident data model expert Viktor, and answered any questions about the designs. One of the features on Raghu's roadmap was something called "Data Mapper". It was the one thing on the roadmap that didn't have any information associated with it. Viktor suggested I talk to two people to try and find out more about it:

  • Phil, a Data Mapping architect, and
  • TJ an "adapter engineer"

About Data Mapper

It took me a while to wrap my head around what data mapping was, but I finally distilled it down to the following:

Data mapper is a set of spreadsheets that documents how to translate a customer’s data model to BYDM. And then from BYDM to Blue Yonder apps that the customer wants to send data to.

Now, mapping sheets are just documentation. They can’t be used as is. Adapter engineers (like TJ) generate reports and then write adapters on MuleSoft that do the actual mapping.

Problems With Data Mapper

But Data Mapper had some problems

Data Mappings are stored in Google Sheets

Phil and team stores the mapping sheets on Google Sheets. There were 900 sheets at last count (Oct 2021).

Performance

Google sheets were never designed to handle this many spreadsheets. Performance has slowed to a crawl. It can take up to an hour to generate a report for an adapter for TJ.

Competitor

Google is a Blue Yonder competitor. Leadership wants to sunset the mapping sheets in the next couple of years.

MuleSoft

Additionally, MuleSoft has decided to End-of-Life the version we use to create our adapters.

I have to get an MVP of Data Mapper out the door before our MuleSoft adapters and Data Mapping spreadsheets are sunset.
